Environmental Assessment under the National Environmental Policy Act and NEPA. The EA describes environmental effects, alternatives, consultation, public comment, significant effects, purpose and need, affected environment, environmental consequences, coordination, environmental review, schedule, objection period, and a publicly circulated review.

The proposed land exchange requires a public interest determination, equal value appraisal, cash equalization, title evidence, mineral reservation, exchange agreement, notice of exchange proposal, segregation, exchange approval, outstanding rights analysis, feasibility analysis, closing documentation, and Forest Service land-exchange policy support under FSM 5430 and FSH 5409.13.
