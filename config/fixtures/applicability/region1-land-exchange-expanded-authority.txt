Purpose and Need

The USDA Forest Service prepares an environmental assessment for a proposed land exchange. The decision notice and FONSI will document the finding of no significant impact. The exchange case includes an agreement to initiate, notice of exchange proposal, appraisal, title evidence, reservations, outstanding rights, equal value, cash equalization, public interest determination, acquisition, disposal, mineral reservation, easement, and exchange approval.

Affected Environment

The project area is on National Forest System land with forest plan direction, management area standards, desired conditions, guidelines, geographic area direction, overlay requirements, species of conservation concern, and a site-specific forest plan amendment. The area includes an inventoried roadless area, recommended wilderness, a wilderness study area, a Wild and Scenic River corridor, a national trail, a special area, cave resource, and paleontological resource. A National Grassland, Bankhead-Jones lands, acquired lands, and grassland plan direction are also described.

Environmental Consequences

The EA evaluates wetlands, waters of the United States, streams, discharge, dredge, fill, stormwater, NPDES, Section 401 water quality certification, Section 404 Corps permit needs, floodplain occupancy, base flood, flood hazard area, air quality, emissions, prescribed fire smoke, general conformity, nonattainment area, state implementation plan, and air quality permit coordination.
